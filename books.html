Alexandra Tsai

BOOKS

Cyberlaw & Order: The Case of the Vanishing Cookies

A thrilling deep dive into the legal intricacies of online privacy, where Alex explores the mystery of disappearing data and the ethical responsibilities of tech companies. A must-read for anyone who thinks cookies are just for eating.

I, Attorney: A Lawyer’s Guide to Surviving in a Digital World

A tongue-in-cheek manual for lawyers navigating the complexities of a digital landscape. Alex provides practical advice on everything from handling virtual hearings to drafting legally sound smart contracts—with plenty of humor along the way.

Ctrl + Alt + Sue: The Future of Litigation in Cyberspace

An exploration of the future of online disputes, where Alex predicts how legal battles will be fought in virtual courts. Featuring scenarios where avatars argue cases, and the gavel might be a mouse click.

The IT’s Legal: Bridging the Gap Between Coders and Courts

In a world where coders and legal professionals often find themselves speaking entirely different languages, Alex’s book provides the ultimate translator. With a blend of humor and deep insight, Alex demystifies legal jargon for techies and untangles the complexities of code for lawyers. Through real-world case studies, this book illustrates how misunderstandings between these two worlds can lead to costly mistakes—and how they can be avoided. Whether you’re a lawyer trying to understand what ‘blockchain’ really means or a developer who just received a 30-page contract to sign, this guide makes it all make sense

Debugging the Law: When Code and Code of Conduct Collide

This book bridges the gap between legal systems and software development, offering insights on how to draft contracts that work like flawless code. Alex’s humorous anecdotes from courtrooms and coding marathons alike keep things lively.

The Law According to Algorithms: When AI Makes the Rules

A forward-looking analysis of how algorithms are beginning to shape legal decisions, and what this means for the future of justice. Alex’s humorous take on whether robots will one day argue cases adds a lighthearted touch to a serious subject.

Objection! Psychology in the Courtroom of Your Mind

A witty exploration of how psychological principles can influence legal decisions and tech innovations. Alex presents case studies that are as enlightening as they are entertaining, showing how your brain might be the ultimate jury.

404: Law Not Found – Legal Challenges in the Age of AI

Alex tackles the legal gray areas created by artificial intelligence, exploring the ethical dilemmas and proposing frameworks for future legislation. Includes a chapter dedicated to why robots might need lawyers, too.
